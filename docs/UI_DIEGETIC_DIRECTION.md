# UI Diegetic Direction

## Intent

Hinomori's UI should feel like a quiet layer of the school world, not a separate game overlay. Most play should happen through the map, prompts, lockers, desks, notes, and arcade cabinets. Screen UI exists only when the world needs a readable close-up.

Reference mockups and games such as Gakuran are inspiration for restraint, sparse HUD, and social-atmosphere framing. Do not copy their layouts, stats screens, phone grid, reroll framing, or monetization language.

## Surface Rules

- **Tiny HUD:** only day phase, school/location, and time flavor. Keep it top-left, text-first, and easy to ignore.
- **Student phone:** social and utility actions such as snack sharing, future contacts, camera, messages, and small notifications.
- **School paper/card:** transfer form, homeroom card, day card, board notices, and future first-day guidance.
- **Locker/world surfaces:** locker notes should look like paper attached to a locker; arcade menus should feel like cabinet glass or an arcade status screen.
- **Gameplay overlays:** rhythm gameplay may use direct screen UI for responsiveness, but should still share the Hinomori palette and avoid generic sci-fi panels.

## Visual Language

- Warm paper, pencil graphite, faded ink, soft black translucent glass, and pale school accents.
- Simple marks only for prototype decoration: lines, stamps, small stickers made from basic UI shapes or text.
- No final Japanese signage, custom icons, textures, or illustrations are required for this slice.
- Avoid heavy rounded cards, bright saturated game panels, big quest banners, and permanent screen clutter.

## Hard Boundaries

- No monetization UI in V0.
- No paid random identity or reroll language.
- No quest/reward treadmill framing.
- No friend free-text, offline notes, or new social persistence.
- Do not use mockup images as production assets.

## Current Slice

Slice 5 creates the shared UI primitives and converts the V0 surfaces: HUD/session, transfer form, locker, snack hand-off phone, arcade cabinet menu, match result slip, and light rhythm gameplay styling. It should not change server authority, persistence, rewards, or gameplay flow.
